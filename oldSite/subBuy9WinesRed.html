The Valtellina area in the Lombardy region

The Alpine valley of Valtellina is situated at the extreme North of Lombardy, between the border with Switzerland and the North side of the Lake of Como, around the city of Sondrio. Famous for its old history and natural beauties, it is also one of the richest Italian regions in terms of food and wine. The "grape" of the area is Nebbiolo, characterising the two local DOCGs: Valtellina Superiore and Sfursat di Valtellina. Those wines present a vigorous character, intense flavours and aromas and a rich palate of vanilla, dried fruits and spices. Very often those wines see oak and have long ageing processes which only end in the bottle after several years.

A bottle of these DOCGs can be kept, if properly stored, for and over a decade, developing always new characteristics.

The wine regions of Tuscany

Tenuta Tignanello, a small gem in the heart of Tuscany

Tenuta Tignanello owned by renown Italian winemaker Antinori is one of the best examples of Super Tuscan. The vineyards grow between the valley of Greve in Chianti and Pesa, in the hearth of the Chianti Classico DOCG area. This wonderful red is the product of the best vintages of native Sangiovese and non-native Cabernet Sauvignon and Cabernet Franc, planted in the Tenuta by Antinori at the end of WWII.

The area of Montalcino

The historical site of Montalcino is famous for its winemaking tradition as since 1888 the best vineyards and vintages of the area are blended to make Brunello di Montalcino wine. Brunello, the local nickname for Sangiovese grape, is often compared to the best Burgundy Pinot Noirs and it must be vinified only from selected Sangiovese grapes of the area and aged at least 2 years in oak barrels and 4 months in bottle. Today 200, among small cantine and family estates, area allowed to produce a very limited amount of this rare and unique product.

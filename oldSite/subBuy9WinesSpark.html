The Prosecco DOCG area in the Veneto Region.

Even if Prosecco grapes are grown and vinified all around the area surrounding Treviso, the finest grapes are found in the small region between Valdobbiadene and Conegliano with point of excellence in the Cartizze area which gives the best examples of this variety.

Prosecco, vinified by the tank method or Charmat-Martinotti method, is a gentle sparkling wine, characterised by a fruity, biscuity and refreshing flavour.

It beautifully accompanies seafood, light fish dishes, pizza and it is the perfect match to Aperol and a slice of orange in famous Italian aperitivo cocktail "Spritz".

Franciacorta Bellavista Cuvee Brut DOCG

Grapes: 80% Chardonnay, 10% Pinot Noir, 10% Pinot Bianco

Alcohol: 11%

Size bottle: 750ml

Price: £37.00

 

One of the best examples of this style, Bellavista is a a mix of horizontal and vertical blends which accounts up to 30 selected white wins from different top vineyards and vintages, some of which passed a short period in oak barrels to add more complexity. It comes with a light straw yellow colour with greenish reflections. Apple, citrus and pear notes are accompanied by a characteristic bread crust flavour and a hint of vanilla. Lively and persistent mousse with a fine and continuous perlage and a crisp finish.

 

Franciacorta Contadi Castaldi DOCG

Grapes: 80% Chardonnay, 10% Pinot Noir, 10% Pinot Bianco

Alcohol: 13%

Size bottle: 750ml

Price: £28.50

 

This is a lively brut, full of fragrance, tending to smoothness and an accessible sensory language, with a balanced and versatile style. In the glass it is yellow in colour with greenish reflections, enlivened by a fine and persistent perlage. The nose is fresh, with floral notes of lime, shades of citrus, white peach and green pepper. The mouth is tight and crisp, fresh and vertical, full-bodied, refined and with a lingering very enjoyable finish. 

 

 

The Franciacorta DOCG area in the Lombardy region.

In this small area near the city of Brescia, a small group of winemakers found the ideal conditions to produce a wine which can often compete easily with French Champagne.

Mainly made from a blend of Chardonnay and Pinot Noit (with  few exceptions to Pinot Bianco), Franciacorta DOCG is one of the finest sparkling wines in the world, vinified by the traditional method or Metodo Classico or Methode Champenoise.

Characterised by a fruity, yeasty flavour, a soft mousse and a fine perlage, Franciacorta wines are aged in bottles with yeast from 18 up to 72 months for the best cru.

It matches a wide ranges of dishes and it is wonderful on its own.
